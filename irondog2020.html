IRONDOG 2020

Track Tim as he attempts the Irondog snowmobile race on foot

The 2409-mile snowmobile race route is the longest snowmobile race in the world. Starting February 1, 2020, Tim Hewitt is attempting to do the trail on foot pulling his supplies. The Irondog course winds through the Alaskan interior from Fairbanks on frozen rivers and through passes to the coast before turning north and after crossing Norton Sounds heads above the artic circle before returning to Nome. From Nome the course follows the 1000 mile Iditarod Trail to the finish in Wassilla AK.  Tim will start in the heart of winter, two weeks before the snowmobile race to optimize his chances for a trail for the harshest part of the expedition above the arctic circle.
